Community Development Services

The Community Development Services (CDS) program provides participants with the opportunity to develop and maintain skills to be productive community members by engaging them in community- based activities alongside people without disabilities.

Scope
  • Day habilitative services, separate from community residential services that can be provided in various settings in the community, based on the person’s interests, skills, and abilities
  • Help participants learn new skills and keep existing skills related to their individual goals and outcome.
  • Facilitate the development of community membership and the opportunities to develop skills and increase independence related to community integration with mostly people without disabilities and paid staff
  • Use different approaches that provide participants with opportunities to engage in community-based activities that support building social connections, employment, socialization, life-long learning, recreation, personal development as well as integration and inclusion in the community.
  • Support the participants’ growth and development of skills and social supports needed for gaining, retaining or advancing competitive integrated employment opportunities.
  • Through support, participants have opportunities to learn socially acceptable behavior and self-advocacy skills (through self-advocacy classes and activities) designed to help them become more independent and to be engaged in their community of choice and reduce or eliminate ongoing staff support.
