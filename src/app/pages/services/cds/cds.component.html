Community Development Services

The Community Development Services (CDS) program provides participants with the opportunity to develop and maintain skills to be productive community members by engaging them in community- based activities alongside people without disabilities.

Scope
  • Day habilitative services, separate from community residential services that can be provided in various settings in the community, based on the person’s interests, skills, and abilities
  • Help participants learn new skills and keep existing skills related to their individual goals and outcome.
  • Facilitate the development of community membership and the opportunities to develop skills and increase independence related to community integration with mostly people without disabilities and paid staff
  • Use different approaches that provide participants with opportunities to engage in community-based activities that support building social connections, employment, socialization, life-long learning, recreation, personal development as well as integration and inclusion in the community.
  • Support the participants’ growth and development of skills and social supports needed for gaining, retaining or advancing competitive integrated employment opportunities.
  • Through support, participants have opportunities to learn socially acceptable behavior and self-advocacy skills (through self-advocacy classes and activities) designed to help them become more independent and to be engaged in their community of choice and reduce or eliminate ongoing staff support.
Scope
  • Promote positive-growth and develop skills and social supports to gain, retain or advance competitive integrated employment opportunities.
  • Provide a setting for participants to learn socially acceptable behavior
  • Support participants efforts in learning, through classes and activities, necessary skills for self-advocacy.
  • Participants shall learn, practice, and apply skills and participate in activities that facilitate and promote greater independence, integration and inclusion in the chosen community. This shall include travel training; using different modes of transportation to, from, and within activities, and identifying a path to employment for those at working age.
  • Building and strengthening relationships with community members who are not paid to be with the participant.
  • Meeting new people, making friends, going to classes to learn, and engaging in activities for fun and fitness.
  • Participating in various local community events, volunteering within a non-profit organization whose mission the participant supports.
  • Training and supports designed to maintain abilities and to prevent or slow loss of skills for participants with declining conditions.
  • Provide personal care assistance (activities of daily living and instrumental activities of daily living) if it does not comprise the entirety of the CDS during community activities.
  • Possible activities include identifying the path to employment, travel training, self-advocacy, local community events and volunteering, retirement activities.
  • Required skills to successfully perform assigned tasks.
  • Community mobility training to eliminate or reduce access limitation to available resources, including transportation.
  • Guidance and training in acceptable community behaviors.
